# Asa: A Browser-Based Forensic Data Integrity Screener for Clinical Trial Datasets

Mahmood Ahmad^1 | ^1 Royal Free Hospital, London, UK | mahmood.ahmad2@nhs.net | ORCID: 0009-0003-7781-4478

## Abstract

Asa (Arabic: Staff of Moses) is a single-file browser application (1,930 lines) that screens clinical trial datasets for statistical signatures of data fabrication or falsification. The tool implements seven forensic methods: Benford's law analysis (first and second digit), GRIM test (granularity-related inconsistency of means), SPRITE (sample parameter reconstruction), terminal digit analysis, variance ratio tests, distribution shape tests (Kolmogorov-Smirnov), and correlation structure anomaly detection. Users paste or upload summary statistics, and Asa produces a forensic integrity report with traffic-light risk indicators. The tool is designed for journal editors, ethics committees, and systematic reviewers performing data quality checks. Available at https://github.com/mahmood726-cyber/asa.

## Introduction

Research integrity concerns in clinical trials have grown as high-profile retractions (Surgisphere, Boldt) demonstrate the consequences of fabricated data reaching systematic reviews. Statistical forensic methods can detect anomalies invisible to peer review, but existing tools are fragmented across R packages (statcheck, scrutiny) and Python scripts. Asa consolidates seven complementary methods into one accessible browser tool.

## Implementation

The engine processes study-level summary statistics (means, SDs, sample sizes, p-values) through a forensic pipeline. Each method produces an independent risk score (0-100). The composite integrity score weights methods by their diagnostic accuracy for fabrication detection (informed by Carlisle 2017, Brown & Heathers 2017). All computation runs client-side in JavaScript. The interface uses a tabbed layout with per-method results and an overall forensic summary panel. Dark mode is default with light mode available.
